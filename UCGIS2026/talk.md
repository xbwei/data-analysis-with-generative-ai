# UCGIS 2026 Lightning Talk

## Building an AI Teaching Stack for GeoAI Education

**Presenter:** Dr. Xuebin Wei  
**Format:** 10-minute lightning talk

---

## Core Message

AI in education should not be presented as a single chatbot. A more useful model is a small, connected teaching stack that supports three modes:

1. **Study Mode** — students ask questions and receive grounded, course-specific guidance.
2. **Teaching Mode** — instructors generate notes, labs, quizzes, images, and short videos more efficiently.
3. **Analysis Mode** — AI-assisted workflows summarize data, support lightweight research tasks, and automate repeatable steps.

The stack combines course content, structured data, AI assistants, and workflow automation while preserving instructor oversight.

---

## Suggested 10-Minute Slide Flow

### Slide 1 — Title and Problem (0:00–0:45)

**Building an AI Teaching Stack for GeoAI Education**

Key question:

```text
How can instructors move beyond isolated chatbots and build a practical, reusable AI teaching workflow?
```

### Slide 2 — From Chatbot to Teaching Stack (0:45–1:45)

Show a simple architecture diagram:

```text
Course Content + Data
        ↓
AI Assistants / Knowledge Retrieval
        ↓
Study Mode | Teaching Mode | Analysis Mode
        ↓
Instructor Review and Human Approval
```

Emphasize that the goal is not full automation. The goal is a modular, instructor-controlled stack.

### Slide 3 — Study Mode (1:45–3:15)

Examples:

- Course-specific chatbot.
- Retrieval from trusted course materials.
- Guided student questions.
- Evidence-grounded responses.
- Optional links back to tutorials, notebooks, and videos.

### Slide 4 — Teaching Mode (3:15–5:15)

Examples:

- Generate lecture notes and lab instructions.
- Draft quizzes and reflection questions.
- Produce diagrams, images, and short videos.
- Turn a workflow into reusable teaching assets.
- Use instructor review before publishing.

### Slide 5 — Analysis Mode (5:15–6:45)

Examples:

- Natural-language queries over structured data.
- AI-assisted interpretation of charts and dashboards.
- Social media knowledge graphs and GraphRAG.
- Automated, repeatable workflows for instructional data analysis.

### Slide 6 — Lightweight Workflow Automation (6:45–8:00)

Examples:

- Trigger an AI workflow.
- Generate a draft.
- Review the result.
- Approve before publication or release.

Highlight human-in-the-loop design.

### Slide 7 — Practical Guardrails (8:00–9:15)

Use four concise points:

```text
Ground responses in trusted materials.
Keep sensitive data private.
Require instructor review for high-impact outputs.
Use AI to support judgment, not replace it.
```

### Slide 8 — Closing (9:15–10:00)

Closing message:

```text
The next step is not adding more chatbots.
It is connecting assistants, content, data, and workflows into a small teaching stack that instructors can actually manage.
```
